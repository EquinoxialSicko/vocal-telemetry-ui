Vocal Telemetry Scale (1-10)

1 - Absolute Resonance: Perfect diaphragmatic support (Nabhi). Seamless movement across saptaks, zero neck tension.

2 - Micro-Friction: Mostly clean phonation. Slight breathiness/air escaping on sustained notes, but pitch remains perfectly anchored.

3 - Abdominal Over-Pressurization: Pushing too hard from the diaphragm to force heavy folds to vibrate. Tone is loud/heavy, but taans feel sluggish.

4 - Nasal Compensation: Pushing sound into the nasal cavity to avoid chest fatigue. Tone becomes thin/whiny; faking high shrutis.

5 - Passaggio Instability: Precarious upper register. Wobbling pitch requiring active physical manipulation of jaw/breath to prevent a crack.

6 - The Falsetto Flip: Involuntary register shifting. Chest register taps out, voice breaks into a weak, hollow falsetto.

7 - Mechanical Fracture: Voice audibly cracks, stutters, or drops out. Continuous glides (Meend) break into jagged steps.

8 - Glottal Squeezing: Abandoned stomach support. Squeezing neck muscles (throat singing). Harsh, tight, strident tone.

9 - Vocal Choking: Severe supraglottic constriction. Wide Akar feels strangled. Forced to unconsciously modify vowels to get sound out.

10 - The Hard Stop: Physical discomfort, dull aching, burning (sandpaper feeling), or total pitch loss. Immediate session stop required.

Vocal Hydration Scale (1-10)

1 - Optimal Viscosity: Perfect mucosal lubrication. Vocal folds are coated in a thin, watery layer. Absolute zero friction. Fast taans and high-register notes slide effortlessly.

2 - Functional Moisture: Clean phonation and resonance, but requires occasional swallowing or a sip of room-temperature water between exercises to maintain the glide.

3 - Mild Tackiness: The mucus layer is starting to thicken slightly. Tone is mostly clean, but the very top of the upper register (Taar Saptak) feels slightly hesitant or requires a tiny bit more breath pressure.

4 - Phlegmatic Coating: "Morning voice" territory. Folds feel slightly puffy or coated in thick mucus. Requires an extended, slow warm-up (like low Kharaj practice) to thin the mucus out.

5 - Friction Onset: Noticeable localized dryness. You feel a slight "tickle" in the throat or an urge to clear your throat/cough after executing heavy glides (meend).

6 - Sticky Phonation: The mucus is thick, sticky, and actively resisting vibration. The folds struggle to cleanly peel apart, resulting in a muffled, veiled, or slightly delayed tone.

7 - Scratchy Vibration: Dryness is actively impeding the hardware. Singing feels audibly scratchy. The highest frequencies begin to drop out completely because the dry folds physically cannot vibrate fast enough.

8 - Systemic Dryness: "Cotton mouth" and dry throat. Saliva is noticeably thick. Swallowing feels dry. Attempting to sing requires pushing through immediate, uncomfortable friction.

9 - Abrasive Scraping: The protective mucosal boundary is severely compromised. The vocal folds are essentially hitting each other bare. Even normal speaking feels harsh, raspy, and fatiguing.

10 - Desiccated (The Desert): Absolute sandpaper. Zero functional lubrication. Attempting to sing physically hurts and guarantees tissue inflammation. Mandatory hard stop and heavy systemic rehydration needed.
